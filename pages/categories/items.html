In Elden Ring, armor is a key component of character customization and survivability, offering protection against physical and elemental damage while also influencing a characterâ€™s mobility and poise. Armor is divided into four main categories: Helmets, Chest Armor, Gauntlets, and Leg Armor, which can be equipped individually to tailor defensive stats and aesthetics.

Armor sets vary widely in weight, defense, and resistances, with heavier sets providing more protection and poise at the cost of mobility and stamina consumption. Lighter armor allows for faster dodging and less encumbrance but offers less defense. The game features a load system that affects roll speed and movement, encouraging players to balance protection and agility based on their build and playstyle. Unlike weapons, armor pieces cannot be upgraded, but their effects can synergize with talismans, spells, or weapon affinities.

Some armor sets offer unique passive effects or lore significance, often tied to NPCs or specific questlines. Players can find armor throughout the Lands Between, either from exploration, merchant purchases, enemy drops, or completing certain quests.
