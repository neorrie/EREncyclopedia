CLASSES

Elden Ring classes represent the various combat roles players can select during Character Creation. Each class sets the foundation for your initial Stats and starting Gear. This guide outlines every available class, detailing their stat distribution and the equipment they begin with.

CLASSES OVERVIEW

WHICH CLASS DO I START WITH?

For newcomers to Elden Ring, well-balanced classes are a solid choice. As a beginner, you’ll need to figure out your preferred playstyle, how you want to tackle challenges, and how to develop your character over time. While each class starts with unique gear, most of this equipment can be acquired later in the game regardless of your initial choice. What truly matters are the base stats and strengths, as these shape your early-game experience. Think about whether you enjoy melee, ranged, or magic combat, and choose a build that aligns with that. Remember, your starting class doesn’t lock you in—gear found along the way and stat upgrades from leveling can significantly alter how you fight.

BUILD TIPS

As you explore Elden Ring, many players recommend trying out beginner-friendly Builds tailored to specific Attributes or playstyles. These pre-made setups are great for getting started, offering balanced approaches for melee, hybrid, or magic-focused characters. For example, the Vagabond offers high survivability and is ideal for those who want to get up close in battle. The Confessor provides a mix of melee and faith-based spells, perfect for players interested in hybrid casting. The Samurai stands out for its versatility and ranged options thanks to its Longbow, while the Astrologer caters to those who prefer casting spells from a distance. Players who enjoy fast-paced, aggressive combat might gravitate towards dual-wielding classes like those with curved swords. Whatever path you choose, don’t be afraid to tweak your Build over time to better match your evolving playstyle.
