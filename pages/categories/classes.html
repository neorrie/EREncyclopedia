CLASSES

In Elden Ring, your starting class determines your character’s initial stats and gear. Each class serves as a starting point for different combat styles, whether it’s close-range melee, spellcasting, or a mix of both. This section provides an overview of all available classes, their starting attributes, and their default equipment.

CLASSES OVERVIEW

WHICH CLASS DO I START WITH?

If you're new to Elden Ring, it's a good idea to pick a class with balanced stats to give yourself flexibility while learning the game’s systems. Your choice won’t permanently define your character, but it will shape your early-game experience. As you level up, you can adjust your stats and equip different gear, allowing you to move toward your preferred playstyle—be it melee-focused, ranged, or magic-oriented.

Choose a class based on what type of gameplay you enjoy: Like fighting up close? Look for a class with high Strength or Vigor. Interested in spellcasting? A class with high Intelligence or Faith is a good start. Want to stay flexible? Hybrid builds or classes with balanced stats offer room to experiment.

Keep in mind: most weapons and gear can be found later on, so your starting loadout isn’t permanent.

BUILD TIPS

As you explore the world, you’ll discover different playstyles that might appeal to you. Trying out a build tailored to your preferred attributes—like Strength for heavy melee or Dexterity for fast attacks—can help make the game more approachable. Feel free to evolve your build over time. The game rewards experimentation and adapting to what you find fun and effective.
